# Data Interpretation

**Data Interpretation** is the step where you **translate** analytical results into **meaningful insight** to make decisions.

It's understanding, validating, and explaining the outputs of models and analyses  in a way that leads to actionable conclusions.

### Explain Results
You need to interpret and understand what do the model results actually mean.

### Evaluate Reliability
Take into account the accuracy, bias, overfitting and precision of the model you're using.

### Extract Insights 
Look for patterns, relationships, anomalies, and make reports.
 
### Connect to Context
Relate to real world context.

## Data Storytelling

**Data Storytelling** consists in communicating the resulting information through a story or narration. It's a very useful way to get to a broader audience, as humans retain more information if it's provided through a story. Data storytelling is composed of three elements:
- **Data**: the actual dataset, that must be clear, ordered and precise.
- **Visualization**: Used to represent said data, using graphical elements to summarize and understand patterns.
- **Narrative**: The story, built around graphical elements, to convey the extracted information to the public.

## Infographic

An infographic is a visual representation of information, data or knowledge, that is designed to present complex information in clear and concise ways.

An infographic is used to:
- present reports **visually**
- highlight **important** data
- to make concepts easier to understand
- promote products or services through **visual marketing**

- Canva
- Infogram
- Piktochart
- Figma
- Lopaka

## Dashboards

**Analytics Dashboards** are visualization tools that show key data and metrics in a single page. This allows users to monitor different aspects of their business/project in real time.

Most dashboards are made with Power BI, Tableau, Looker Studio or `matplotlib`.
